When we add plasticisers to the polymer, the Tg value is
ChatGPT said:

Good question 👍

🔹 Effect of Plasticisers on TgT_gTg​ (Glass Transition Temperature):

  • Plasticisers are low-molecular-weight additives added to polymers to make them more flexible and softer.

  • They work by reducing intermolecular forces between polymer chains and increasing free volume.

  • As a result, chain mobility increases.

👉 Therefore, the glass transition temperature (TgT_gTg​) decreases when plasticisers are added.

✅ Final Answer:
When we add plasticisers to a polymer, the TgT_gTg​ value decreases.

🔹 Effect of Bulky Groups on TgT_gTg​

  • Bulky side groups (like –C(CH₃)₃ in polystyrene) restrict the rotation and mobility of polymer chains.

  • This makes the chains stiffer and reduces their ability to move past one another.

  • Since TgT_gTg​ depends on chain mobility, restricted mobility → higher TgT_gTg​.

✅ Final Answer:
When bulky groups are present in a polymer, the TgT_gTg​ value increases.
